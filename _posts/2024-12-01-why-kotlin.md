Everyone's talking about Flutter. Or React Native. Or the next cross-platform solution.

I'm still writing Kotlin.

## The Boring Answer

It works. Really well.

After 12 years of Android development, I can build anything in Kotlin. Fast. Clean. Maintainable.

Why would I switch?

## The Real Answer

Kotlin is beautiful.

```kotlin
// This is valid Kotlin
users
    .filter { it.age >= 18 }
    .map { it.name }
    .forEach { println(it) }
```

It reads like thought. No ceremony. No noise.

## What About Flutter?

I use Flutter too. Built parts of Tamara's app with it. Migrating Cashia to it.

Flutter is great for what it is - a cross-platform UI toolkit.

But Kotlin is a language. A way of thinking.

## The Ecosystem

Android's ecosystem is mature:
- Jetpack Compose (finally, good UI)
- Coroutines (async that makes sense)
- Room (SQLite without pain)
- Hilt (DI that doesn't suck)

Everything just works. No fighting the framework.

## Performance Still Matters

Native is native. 

When you're handling payments, every millisecond counts. When you're tracking location for delivery drivers, battery efficiency matters.

Kotlin gives me that control.

## The Future

Will I be writing Kotlin in 2030? Maybe not.

But the patterns I've learned, the architecture principles, the way of thinking - that transfers.

Good code is good code, regardless of language.

## My Advice

Learn one thing deeply before chasing the next.

I know developers who've switched frameworks five times in five years. They can build demos in everything but production apps in nothing.

Pick a stack. Master it. Ship products.

The technology doesn't matter as much as what you build with it.

---

*Kotlin isn't trendy anymore. It's just good. Sometimes that's enough.*
